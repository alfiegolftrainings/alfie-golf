This week's focus
Ball-first contact with irons and wedges

Joe, consistent iron and wedge striking is one of the most rewarding things to improve — and at 24.9, cleaning up your contact will drop shots faster than almost anything else. This week is entirely about finding the bottom of your swing arc in the right place, so you're hitting ball-first more often.

I'm keeping this to one focused drill — that's intentional. Thirty minutes of deep, deliberate work on one thing will do far more for your striking than rushing through three drills.

Drills
1
Step-Through Towel Drill
25 min
What to do
1. Grab your pitching wedge and a 7-iron. You'll use both during this drill.

2. Lay a small towel flat on the ground about 2–3 inches behind the ball. The towel is your feedback tool — if you catch it, your low point is too far back.

3. Set up to the ball normally. On the downswing, focus on shifting onto your lead foot and stepping through after impact — your trail foot should step forward past your lead foot, like you're walking toward the target. This forces your weight forward and moves the low point ahead of the ball.

4. Hit 15 shots with the pitching wedge. Your goals: miss the towel completely, feel a clean ball-first strike, and finish with that step-through toward the target. Listen for a crisp "click" rather than a heavy thud.

5. After each shot, note: did you catch the towel or miss it? Use the range tracking system to watch your carry distances — as your strike cleans up, you'll see those numbers tighten.

6. Once you've hit 15 PW shots, switch to the 7-iron and hit 15 more with the same step-through feel and towel placement.

7. That's 30 balls total. No need to rush — take your time between shots and really commit to the step-through on every rep.
Why this matters for you
At a 24.9 handicap, the most common reason irons and wedges feel inconsistent is that the low point of the swing is landing behind the ball — producing fat shots, thin shots, and unpredictable distances. The towel gives you instant feedback on where your club is bottoming out, and the step-through forces your weight forward so the low point moves to the right spot. You don't need a swing overhaul — you need your body ahead of the ball at impact. The tracking system in your range bay is a huge advantage here — use it to watch your carry distances tighten up as your strike improves. That consistency is what turns a 24.9 into something much lower.
