This week's focus
Diagnosing your driver miss pattern and building a dependable fairway-wood tee shot

Tanner, hitting 3 off the tee four or more times a round is costing you 4–8 strokes minimum — that alone could get you from 100 into the low 90s without changing anything else in your bag. You already know early extension is the culprit, and since you manage it with irons and wedges, the issue is likely amplified by the longer shaft, lower loft, and the instinct to swing harder with the driver. This week we're going to attack this from two angles: finding out exactly what your driver miss looks like, and building a reliable tee shot with your fairway woods that you can trust on the course right now.

Drills
1
Driver Gate Drill
25 min
What to do

Tee up a ball with your driver. About 3 feet in front of your ball, push two tees into the ground to form a gate just wider than the width of a clubhead — roughly 6 inches apart. The gate should be centred on your target line. Hit 30 drivers through the gate at no more than 80% effort. For each shot, note the result: through the gate, missed left of the gate, or missed right of the gate. Don't try to steer the ball — swing normally but at controlled speed. After 30 balls, tally your totals: how many through, how many left, how many right. This tells us exactly what your miss pattern is and gives us a baseline to track week over week.

Why this matters for you

When you're re-teeing 4+ times a round, it's easy to think the driver is just broken. But the miss has a pattern — it almost always favours one side, and early extension typically sends the ball in a predictable direction. This drill strips away the pressure of distance and course management and isolates the one question that matters right now: where is the clubhead actually going at impact? With two sessions a week, you'll have 60 gate swings of data by next week, and we'll know exactly what to work on in Week 2.

2
Fairway Finder — Woods Only, No Driver
25 min
What to do

Grab your 3-wood or 5-wood (whichever you have more confidence in — if you have both, use the higher-lofted one). Tee the ball up low or place it on the ground, and position it in the middle of your stance rather than off your front heel. Pick a target on the range and imagine a fairway 30 yards wide centred on that target. Hit 30 shots at 75–80% swing speed. The goal is not distance — it's finding that 30-yard corridor. For each shot, call it a fairway hit or a miss. Focus on a smooth, controlled tempo and a balanced finish — you should be able to hold your finish for a full second on every swing.

Why this matters for you

At 95–105 strokes, having a reliable tee shot you trust is more valuable than having a long tee shot you don't. Early extension tends to get worse the longer the club and the harder you swing — so a fairway wood with the ball centred and the effort dialled back gives you the best chance of staying in posture naturally, without even thinking about it. This drill builds a shot you can take to the course immediately on any hole where driver feels risky. Over time, as your gate drill numbers improve, the driver will earn its way back into more holes.
