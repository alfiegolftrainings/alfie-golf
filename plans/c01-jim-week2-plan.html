1
Headcover Path Drill — Driver
25 min
What to do

Place a headcover on the ground just outside the ball and slightly behind it (a touch further from the target than the ball). The headcover acts as a guard — if your path is over the top or outside-in, you'll clip it. Your job is to swing from inside-to-outside, missing the headcover, while keeping the clubface square to the target. Hit 25 balls with your driver. Pick a fairway-width target on the range — roughly 40 yards wide. Before each shot, visualise a fairway corridor and commit to a start line. Count how many of the 25 land inside that 40-yard corridor.

Why this matters for you

You said driving has gotten worse since the start of the year. At 10.8, finding fairways is where you protect your scorecard — missed fairways at your level usually mean bogey or worse. This drill attacks the most common cause of offline tee shots: an outside-in path that produces weak slices or pulled drives. The headcover gives you instant feedback on every swing without needing a launch monitor. If you're catching it, you know exactly what to fix. Keep the face square and let the path do the work.

2
18-Hole Up-and-Down Challenge
35 min
What to do

Place 18 balls around the chipping green in different positions — vary the lies, distances, and angles. Include at least 3–4 balls in the bunker. For each ball, choose the same hole (or rotate holes if you prefer — but commit before you drop the ball). Par for each "hole" is 2 strokes: 1 chip/bunker shot + 1 putt. Play all 18 and keep a running score just like on the course. Par for the round is 36. Use whatever club you'd normally choose on the course for each situation — bump-and-runs, lobs, bunker shots, all fair game.

Why this matters for you

You flagged chipping as an area that's slipped since the start of the year. This drill forces you to read lies, pick shots, and execute under light scoring pressure — exactly what you face on the course. At your handicap, converting more up-and-downs is one of the fastest routes to dropping shots. The variety of lies and the bunker shots stop it from becoming repetitive and build the adaptability you need across a real round.
