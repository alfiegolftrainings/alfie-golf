Ryan, you've flagged two areas that are eating your scores — wedges and the tee box. This week I'm giving you one drill for each, but here's the key: focus on only one drill per session. Don't try to cram both into 30 minutes. Session 1, pick one. Session 2, pick the other. That single-focus approach will get you further than splitting your attention ever will.

Drills
1
Distance Ladder — Wedges
30 min
What to do

Pick your most-used wedge (pitching wedge or sand wedge — whichever you grab most inside 100 yards). Set up at the range and choose four targets at roughly 30, 50, 70, and 90 yards. Hit 5 balls to the 30-yard target, then 5 to 50, then 5 to 70, then 5 to 90. That's 20 balls — one "ladder." Focus on making smooth, controlled swings rather than muscling the ball. After the ladder, repeat with your remaining time. You're learning what a half-swing, three-quarter swing, and full swing actually produce with one club. If you'd rather mix clubs to hit those distances — say, sand wedge for the shorter targets and pitching wedge for the longer ones — go for it. The goal is the same: build a feel for predictable distances with whatever club you choose.

Why this matters for you

At 19.9, a huge chunk of your scoring happens inside 100 yards. Dialling in predictable wedge distances means you're giving yourself more realistic birdie and par looks instead of leaving chips and pitches in no-man's land. This is one of the fastest ways to chip strokes off your handicap.

2
Gate Drill — Driver
30 min
What to do

Place two alignment sticks in the ground about 3 feet in front of your ball, spaced roughly a clubhead-width apart to form a gate. Tee up and hit 20 drives with one goal: start the ball cleanly through the gate. Before every shot, stand behind the ball, pick your line, then step in and go. After each shot, note whether the ball went through the gate, missed left, or missed right. If you're consistently missing one side, don't try to fix your swing mid-drill — just note the pattern and keep committing to your line.

Why this matters for you

You mentioned the tee box as a frustration, and at your handicap, the fastest win off the tee isn't more distance — it's keeping the ball in play. One fewer penalty or punch-out per round can save you 2–3 strokes immediately. The gate gives you instant feedback on your start line, which is the single biggest factor in finding fairways.
