Roy, "sliced drives and bad spots off the tee" is exactly what your last two rounds confirm — a 104 and a 53 on 9 happen when the driver starts the hole in the wrong place and every shot after is damage control. What you mentioned about the irons is important too: toe contact and an extreme slice are almost never separate problems at your level. Both typically come from the same move — the club swinging over the top and the body standing up through impact, which throws the club outward, opens the face, and puts contact toward the toe. This week targets that one thing from two angles. You mentioned you've never followed a structured practice routine before, so this plan keeps it to two focused drills — one clear thing to fix, two ways to feel it. That's intentional. A plan you can actually absorb and repeat three times this week will do far more than five drills you half-remember.

Drills
1
Path gate with headcover — 3W then driver 30 min
What to do

Place a headcover (or a golf bag, towel, or any object roughly fist-sized) on the ground just outside the ball — about 6 inches beyond the ball on the far side from you, just outside the target line. This is your path guard. If you swing over the top, the club head will clip it on the way down. Your job: miss the headcover by swinging the club from the inside. Start with your 3-wood for the first 3 sets (shorter shaft makes it easier to feel the path change), then switch to driver for the last 3 sets. Hit in sets of 5 balls, pause between sets to reset your aim. 6 sets total, 30 balls.

Why this drill for you

At a 21 handicap with an extreme slice, you're losing 20+ yards of effective driving distance because every hole is playing on the diagonal. You mentioned you normally shoot in the 90s — the 104 and 53 happened because the driver put you in spots that turned bogeys into doubles. The headcover gives you instant, honest feedback: you'll hear and feel it when you come over the top, and you'll miss it cleanly when the path is right. No tracking system needed.

Score to record
Clean swings (missed headcover): / 30
2
Posture guard — 7 or 8 iron 20 min
What to do

Set up to hit a normal shot with your 7 or 8 iron. Before you swing, push a tee into the mat using this reference: picture a clock face with the ball at the centre and your body at 6 o'clock. Place the tee at 12 o'clock — directly away from your body, 3 inches from the ball. It should be clearly visible when you look down at address. Now swing. If your body stands up and reaches through impact, the club head swings outward and hits the tee. If you stay in your posture, the club travels on the same radius as at address and misses the tee cleanly. Hit in sets of 3 balls, pause between sets to reset your address. 5 sets, 15 balls total. Ignore the ball flight — focus only on the tee.

Why this drill for you

The toe contact you described on irons is the same move as the slice, showing up differently. When you stand up through impact, the club head is suddenly further from your body than it was at address — it finds the toe instead of the centre. Keeping the tee untouched trains you to stay in your posture and keep the club on the radius it started on. Fix this and the toe contact cleans up; it also reinforces the path you've been working on in Drill 1.

Clean swings (tee untouched): / 15
