J, pulling irons is one of the most frustrating patterns at your handicap — you're striking it well enough to hit greens, but the ball keeps leaking left and you're leaking shots with it. This week we're attacking the root cause: the clubhead overtaking your hands through impact. Two drills, one focused theme.

Drills
1
Ball Gate Drill
30 min
What to do

Place two golf balls on the ground to form a gate your clubhead must pass through. One ball sits outside the target line and slightly ahead of your ball position; the other sits inside the target line and slightly behind your ball position — so the gate runs diagonally and the clubhead can only clear it cleanly if your hands lead through first. Round 1 — Medium gate (15 balls): Set the two gate balls so the gap is roughly 1.5 clubhead widths. Hit 15 shots with a 7-iron, focusing on feeling your hands pass over the gate before the clubhead follows. If you strike either gate ball, it doesn't count as a pass. Round 2 — Tight gate (15 balls): Narrow the gap so it's just barely wider than your clubhead. Same focus — hands leading, clubhead trailing. Hit 15 shots. Between each shot, reset the gate balls precisely. No rushing — the setup is part of the drill.

Why this matters for you

A pulled iron almost always means the clubhead is racing past the hands and the face is closing early. At 15 handicap you're generating enough speed to hit greens — you just need the face square at impact. This gate forces you to feel true shaft lean and hand-lead. If the clubhead overtakes your hands, you'll clip a gate ball immediately. The progression from medium to tight gate builds that feel gradually so it sticks on the course.

2
18-Hole Approach Challenge
30 min
What to do

On the tracking system, load an 18-hole approach game (one shot per hole, varying distances and green sizes). Use the appropriate iron or wedge for each distance — don't default to the same club. Treat every shot like a real approach: pick a specific target on the green, commit to the shot shape, and execute. Carry the hands-first feel from the gate drill into every swing here. Track how many greens you hit out of 18. After each miss, note whether the miss was a pull — this will tell you whether the gate drill feel is transferring.

Why this matters for you

You said it yourself — pulled irons are costing you GIRs. This challenge puts the gate drill feel under realistic pressure with changing distances and targets, exactly like an actual round. At 15 handicap, even gaining 2–3 extra GIRs per round drops real shots off your score. This is where the practice becomes your game.
