Tempo + hands-ahead feel

Joon — I hear you: "Topping balls when I try to hit them too hard." There's no need to force it — if contact is right, the club will do the work. This session trains a hands-ahead feel and controlled tempo so harder intent produces clean, low long-iron contact, not tops.

Drills
1
Tempo ladder — half and three-quarter swings 10 min
What to do

Using a 4- or 5-iron, hit 12 half-swings (finish at waist height) then 12 three-quarter swings (finish at shoulder height). Count "1–2" out loud on every shot to lock a steady tempo — if you feel yourself rushing, stop, take a breath, and reset before the next ball. Check the tracker after each shot: you're looking for a lower, more penetrating ball flight compared to your usual full swing. That's the signal contact is clean.

Why this drill for you

Slowing the tempo removes the rush that causes topping — you're training your body to feel what "in control" looks like before asking it to do more.

Score to record Shots with lower, penetrating flight on tracker: / 24
2
Hands-ahead contact drill 10 min
What to do

With a 5-iron, hit 20 controlled swings while focusing on one feel: hands leading the clubhead through impact, as if you're trying to keep the grip end of the club pointing at your left hip through the hitting zone. Check the tracker after each shot — the goal is the lowest apex you can achieve while still getting reasonable carry. If you start topping, shorten the backswing until contact comes back, then gradually extend again. Record apex only on clean contacts — skip any topped or thinned shots.

Why this drill for you

Feeling the hands lead through impact is the sensation that produces ball-first contact — the direct fix for topping when you try to hit harder.

3
Committed full swings to a landing zone 10 min
What to do

Pick a target on the range and set a carry distance with your 5-iron. Hit 15 full swings to that zone using the same tempo and hands-ahead feel from drills 1 and 2. Use the tracker after each shot and count only balls that land within ±10 yards of your intended carry. If you start topping, go back to the "1–2" count from drill 1 — don't add swing speed, add rhythm.

Why this drill for you

This is where you transfer the tempo and contact feel into a committed full swing — the goal isn't to swing easier, it's to prove you can swing fully without topping when the mechanics are right.

Score to record Shots within ±10 yards of target: / 15
