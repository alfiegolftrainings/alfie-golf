Drills
1
Step-Through Towel Strikes
30 min
What to do

Fold a hand towel and lay it on the hitting strip about 3–4 inches behind the ball. The towel is your guard rail — if you hit fat, you'll catch the towel before the ball and you'll feel it immediately.


Set up to the ball normally, then hit the shot with a step-through finish: as you swing through impact, let your trail foot step forward past your lead foot, like you're walking toward the target. You should finish standing on your lead foot with your trail foot a step ahead of where it started. This forces your weight to move forward through the shot, which moves your low point forward — exactly where it needs to be for clean contact.


Start with your pitching wedge. Hit 10 balls. After each shot, check the Mevo for carry distance and smash factor if available. Write down the carry for each shot. The PW is your calibration club — you should feel confident and in control here.


Move to the 7-iron. Hit 10 balls with the same step-through and towel setup. Record carry distances.


Finish with the 5-iron. Hit 10 balls. Same process. This one will feel the hardest to step through smoothly — that's fine. If the step-through feels too awkward at first, make it smaller, but keep the weight moving forward.


After all 30 balls, look at each club's carry numbers. Circle the shots where your carry was within 5 yards of your average for that club. Those are your "in zone" strikes. Also note how many times you caught the towel.


Between shots, take your time. Reset your feet, place the towel back if it shifted, and do one slow rehearsal step-through without a ball before each real swing.

Why this matters for you
At a 20 handicap with scores ranging from 94 to 110 on the course, the gap between your best and worst rounds is almost certainly tied to how clean you're striking these irons. Your sim handicap being 5 shots lower than your course handicap tells me you can make the swing — but consistency wavers, especially under course pressure. The towel punishes the fat miss instantly so you don't need to guess, and the step-through builds the forward weight shift that makes clean strikes repeatable rather than random. Starting with the PW gives us a baseline where contact should be easy — if the towel catches you even there, we know exactly what to fix. The Mevo gives us real data to track it week over week.
