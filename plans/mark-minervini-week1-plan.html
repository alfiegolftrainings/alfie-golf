Mark — you said it yourself: "Straight drives only 50% of the time and inconsistent ball striking on irons." We'll keep this 30-minute session focused: train a square clubface through a two-ball gate, build repeatable iron contact with a feedback tee, then finish with 20 deliberate driver reps into a defined fairway zone.

Drills
1
Clubhead gate (two-ball gate) 8 min
What to do

Place your ball on the tee or mat. Set two spare balls on the ground just outside the toe and heel of your clubhead — roughly 1–2 inches wider on each side — forming a gate the clubhead must pass through cleanly. Using a 9- or 8-iron, hit 8 shots with the same routine each time (pick a target, set up square, one practice swing, step in). Only count swings where the clubhead passes through without touching either gate ball. If you clip one, don't adjust — just repeat the same setup and focus on the gap.

Why this drill for you

The gate gives you instant feedback on whether your face and path are square at impact, directly targeting your "inconsistent ball striking on irons."

Score to record Gate passes (clean through gap): / 8
2
7-iron contact ladder 10 min
What to do

Place a small tee or coin 1–2 inches behind the ball on the mat — if you hit behind it, you'll feel and hear it immediately. Hit 5 half-swings focusing only on clipping the ball first, then 5 three-quarter or full swings. Use the same short routine before every shot. If a shot feels fat, take a breath, reset your routine, and move on to the next ball.

Why this drill for you

Training yourself to strike the ball before the ground directly attacks your "inconsistent ball striking on irons."

Score to record Solid center strikes (no tee contact): / 10
3
Driver — 20-ball fairway block 12 min
What to do

Pick one target on the range and define a fairway zone about 40 yards wide centred on it — use two visible markers (flags, buckets, fence posts) as your left and right edges. Place a spare club on the ground along your feet for consistent alignment. If you have an alignment stick, push it through the top of a range bucket and place the bucket just outside your rear hip line, pointing down the target line — this gives your swing a visual path to swing past and naturally encourages an inside-to-out movement. Hit exactly 20 driver balls using the same short routine each time (pick target, align, one practice swing, step in). After each shot, mark down only one thing: did it land inside the 40-yard fairway zone?

Why this drill for you

Repeating 20 committed reps into a defined window — with a path reference if you have sticks — directly attacks "Straight drives only 50% of the time."

Score to record Fairways hit: / 20
