Nick, driving is your main frustration — and at 25.2 that's one of the fastest places to drop shots. Fewer drills, more reps, more progress.

Drills
1
Headcover Drill – Driver
30 min
What to do

Place your headcover on the ground just outside the ball (away from you) and slightly behind it — about one inch toward your trail foot. The headcover is a guardrail: if your club swings too far from the inside or your path drifts outward, you'll clip it. Tee the ball at your normal driver height. Hit 30 balls total. Pick a target on the range and imagine a 40-yard-wide fairway around it — that's roughly the width between two range markers one bay apart, or about 20 yards either side of your target line. Before each shot, visualise that corridor. After each swing, note two things: (1) did you miss the headcover? (2) did the ball finish inside your 40-yard fairway? Only count a rep as a success if both are true. Between shots, reset the headcover position carefully — it tends to shift. Take your time with setup; rushing is the enemy here.

Why this matters for you

At 25.2, errant drives aren't just costing you distance — they're costing you penalty strokes and impossible second shots. The headcover placement behind and outside the ball trains you to deliver the club from the inside without over-rotating. Every ball that clears the headcover AND lands in the fairway is proof your path is improving. This is where your handicap starts to fall.

2
Fairway Finder – Woods
30 min
What to do

Put the driver away. Grab your 3-wood or 5-wood — whichever you trust more (or alternate between both). Tee the ball lower than normal — just barely off the ground, so the top of the ball sits level with the top of the clubface. Position the ball in the middle of your stance, not forward like a driver. Now the key: swing at 70–75% effort. Think "smooth and controlled," not "hit." Pick a target on the range and imagine a 30-yard-wide fairway — that's roughly 15 yards either side of your target line. This is a tighter corridor than the driver drill, but you're swinging easier and with a more forgiving club. Hit 30 balls. For each shot, note whether the ball finishes inside that 30-yard fairway. No need for the headcover here — this drill is purely about finding the short grass with a reliable, repeatable swing.

Why this matters for you

Nick, on holes where driver is getting you into trouble, this is your escape plan. A wood off the tee with a controlled swing and a centred ball position will fly straighter and more consistently. At your handicap, a 200-yard shot in the fairway beats a 240-yard shot in the trees every single time. Building trust in this option now gives you a real decision to make on the course — and that's a huge step.
