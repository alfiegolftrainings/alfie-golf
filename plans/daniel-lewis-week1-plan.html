Drills
1
Step and Strike + Towel — 7-iron
25 min
What to do
1. On the grass range, place a small towel flat on the ground a few inches behind the ball — not right against it, but with a clear gap between the towel's edge and the ball. The ball sits on the turf just ahead of the towel.

2. Set up to the ball with your 7-iron, but bring your trail foot in so that both feet start together — side by side, directly beneath you. This is your starting position for every swing.

3. Take your backswing from this narrow stance.

4. As you begin the downswing, step your lead foot out to your normal stance width — planting it firmly in its usual address position as the club comes down. The step is the trigger for the downswing: your lead side lands and stabilises before the club reaches the ball.

5. Complete the swing normally. When the step works, your weight commits forward before impact, the club bottoms out in front of the ball, and the towel stays untouched.

6. Hit 20 balls. After each shot, check one thing: did the towel move? If the club catches it, weight stayed back — slow the swing down, exaggerate the step, and focus on landing the lead foot firmly before the club arrives.
Why this matters for you
The low ball flight, the blocks, and the hooks all share the same origin: weight staying on the trail side through impact, causing the club to bottom out too early and the face to arrive offline. The step drill removes the option to stay back — physically committing your weight forward before the club arrives at the ball. The towel gives you honest, instant feedback on every rep: untouched means the sequence was right. Your 8-iron already carries 140 yards, which means the distance is there. This drill is about making sure the club is delivering at the right moment, which is what turns mid-iron misses into greens.
