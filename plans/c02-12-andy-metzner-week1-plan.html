Alfie Golf Trainings — Practice Plan
Total time
60 min
Location
Driving range (grass tees if available)
This week's focus
Tee shot reliability — contact quality and start-line control

Andy, your frustration is clear — it's not that you can't find a fairway, it's that you can't trust what's coming off the tee. Dropkicks and huge slices are killing your scores, and when you don't know which miss is coming, confidence evaporates. This week we're going to rebuild that trust in three stages: first by simplifying your tee game with fairway woods, then by confronting the driver head-on with a gate that forces you to commit to a start line, and finally by training your low point to eliminate those dropkicks for good.

Drills
1
Fairway Finder — Woods Only
20 min
What to do
Pick a target line on the range that represents a fairway — roughly 30 yards wide. Use only fairway woods (3-wood, 5-wood, 7-wood — whatever you carry, NO driver). Set up with the ball in the middle of your stance, not forward like you would with a driver. This encourages ball-first contact and takes the dropkick out of play. Swing at 70–75% effort — think "controlled tempo," not "hit it far." Hit 20 balls total. Before each shot, pick the centre of your 30-yard fairway and commit to it. After each ball, honestly judge: did it land inside the 30-yard corridor? Track your count out of 20.
Why this matters for you
At a 6 handicap you have a real swing — the issue isn't ability, it's that full-send tee shots are producing two very different misses (dropkicks and huge slices). Ball-middle and 75% effort strips out the two biggest causes: low point falling behind the ball and an over-the-top lunge. You don't need to hit it 280 to score — you need to know where it's going. Building confidence with fairway woods off the tee will immediately stop the handicap from rocketing any further.

2
Driver Gate Drill
20 min
What to do
Now switch to driver. Place two alignment sticks in the ground approximately 3 feet in front of the ball, spaced about 4 feet apart — wide enough that a straight or slightly shaped shot passes through, but narrow enough that a huge slice or a duck hook won't. The sticks should be angled into the ground firmly so they're visible but not in danger of being hit by the clubhead. Hit 20 balls. Your only job is to start the ball through the gate. Don't worry about where it ends up 200 yards away — just through the gate. Track how many pass through, and for any that miss, note whether they went left or right of the gate. This tells you your pattern.
Why this matters for you
You said you'd be happy just knowing the miss is going to be right or left each hole. This drill forces exactly that awareness. By focusing on the first 3 feet of ball flight, you strip away the anxiety about the result and zero in on start line. Over 20 balls, you'll see a clear pattern — if 14 miss right of the gate, that's your slice showing up and you can aim for it. If it's a mix, your swing path is inconsistent and that's what we work on next week. Either way, you leave with data instead of frustration.

3
Headcover Path Drill — Driver
20 min
What to do
Place a headcover on the ground just outside the ball (away from you) and about one inch behind it — so the headcover sits slightly closer to your trail foot than the ball does. Tee up as normal with your driver. Hit 20 balls. Your goal is simple: miss the headcover on every swing. If your club drops behind and outside the ball — the move that causes dropkicks and over-the-top slices — you'll clip the headcover. A clean miss means your low point is forward and your path is working from the inside. If you hit the headcover, no judgement — just reset it and go again. Track how many swings out of 20 you make clean contact without touching the headcover.
Why this matters for you
Your two big misses — dropkicks and huge slices — both start with the club falling behind and outside the ball. The headcover acts as a physical guardrail against exactly that move. At a 6 handicap your swing doesn't need a rebuild, it needs a constraint that stops the bad pattern from firing. This drill gives you instant, unmistakable feedback every rep: if the headcover moves, you know what happened. Over 20 balls you'll start feeling the difference between a clean path and the one that's been costing you fairways.
