Drills
1
Headcover Gate + Ball-Box Drill (Driver)
35 min
What to do

Set up with a modification from last week's Headcover Gate Drill — place a headcover just outside the toe line of your driver, a few inches behind the ball (further from the target), so an over-the-top path that cuts across the ball would strike it on the way down. Tee up your ball as normal. Now add the new element: place a small range ball box (or a cluster of 3–4 range balls) on the ground roughly 2 inches directly in front of your teed-up ball, on the target side. The box should be low enough that a driver swinging upward will clear it, but high enough that a steep, downward hit will clip it. Your job: swing through without touching the headcover behind the ball AND without clipping the box in front. If you hit the headcover, your path is still outside-in — the club is approaching too far from the outside on the downswing. If you hit the box, you're swinging too steeply down — you need to feel like you're sweeping up through the ball. Hit 40 balls in sets of 10. Between each set, step away, reset the station, and note how many clean passes you made (no headcover contact AND no box contact). By the end of last week you were finding draws — now we want those draws launched higher and with less spin, which is what hitting up produces.

Why this matters for you

Last week you proved to yourself that your path can change — you went from slicing to drawing by the end of the session. But at a 28 handicap, the slice will creep back if the path fix isn't paired with the right angle of attack. Most slicers hit down on the driver, which adds loft, adds spin, and makes the slice curve even more. The ball box forces you to shallow out and strike upward. Combined with the headcover gate you already trust — now placed behind the ball where it more directly blocks the outside-in downswing move — this locks in both corrections at once. You said the first drill gave you immediate improvement — this is the upgraded version.

2
Fairway Finder — 3-Wood or Hybrid
20 min
What to do

Pick your 3-wood or hybrid — whichever you'd reach for on a tight tee shot. Tee the ball up low, just barely off the ground. Play the ball in the centre of your stance, not forward like a driver. Pick two targets on the range roughly 30 yards apart to create an imaginary fairway — use flags, markers, or any two reference points you can see. Hit 20 balls. Every shot has one single goal: land it between your two targets. Don't worry about distance, don't chase a draw or a fade — just find the fairway. Make a smooth, controlled swing at about 80% effort. Between each shot, step behind the ball, pick your two targets again, and commit to the corridor before you step in. If you're finding it easy after 10 balls, narrow the fairway by picking targets closer together. If you're struggling, widen it — the goal is to finish the drill feeling like you can aim and deliver.

Why this matters for you

At 28 handicap, keeping the ball in play off the tee is the single fastest way to cut big numbers. You've been focused on fixing the driver slice — and that's working — but having a reliable fairway club you trust gives you an escape valve on the course when the driver isn't cooperating. Centre ball position and a low tee naturally produce a more level strike, which is easier to control than a full driver launch. This drill builds a shot you can take to the course right now while we keep developing your driver.
