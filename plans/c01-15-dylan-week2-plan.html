Drills
1
Pause at the Top — Tempo Drill
25 min
What to do

Pick your 7-iron to start. You'll work in blocks: Block 1: - Hit 10 balls with a deliberate pause at the top of your backswing. Count "one-one-thousand" at the top before starting down. The downswing is triggered by shifting your weight to your lead foot — don't pull with your arms. - Then hit 5 balls with your normal swing, but try to keep the same unhurried tempo you felt during the pause reps. No pause this time — just the feeling. Block 2: - Repeat: 10 balls with the pause, then 5 balls normal swing with that same tempo feeling. That's 30 balls total. On the 10 normal-swing shots (the two sets of 5), pay attention to contact quality — you're looking for that same solid, compressed strike you were nailing last week on the towel drill.

Why this matters for you

At 19.8, inconsistency often comes from rushing the transition — the club never quite gets into the right slot, so even when your swing path is fine, timing is off. Last week proved your contact fundamentals are there (84% clean strikes). Now we're making sure your tempo doesn't undo that good work under pressure. The pause gives your body a checkpoint; the normal swings test whether the feeling sticks.

2
Distance Ladder — Iron Precision
30 min
What to do

You'll hit to four distances: 75, 100, 125, and 150 yards. For each distance, choose whatever club you feel most comfortable will get you there — partial swings are fine and encouraged for the shorter targets. One ladder sequence is: 75 → 100 → 125 → 150 → 150 → 125 → 100 → 75 That's 8 shots per ladder. Complete 4 full ladders = 32 shots total. Before each shot, commit to the distance out loud or in your head: "This is a 100-yard shot." Pick a specific target on the range at that distance. After each shot, judge whether the ball landed within ±5 yards of your intended distance. Tip: Use the tempo feeling from Drill 1. The shorter distances especially reward that unhurried transition.

Why this matters for you

Inconsistency isn't just about contact — it's about knowing your distances and trusting partial swings. You proved last week you can strike it clean with a 9, 7, and 5 iron. Now we're asking: can you control how far those clean strikes go? For a 19.8 handicap, tightening your distance control inside 150 yards is one of the fastest ways to hit more greens and lower scores.
