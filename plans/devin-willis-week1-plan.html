Devin, with how often you play and how clear your goal is around tee shot accuracy, this session has one job: give you a fairway finder you actually trust when the tee shot counts. Two range drills, then a simple thing to track next time you play — which, knowing you, is probably tomorrow.

Drills
1
Fairway finder corridor (3W / 5W) 15 min
What to do

On the range, pick two clear visual markers (flags, distance signs, trees) that create a 30–35 yard "fairway" corridor. Tee the ball low with your 3 or 5 wood only. Hit 3-ball sets: before each set, pick your exact target line in that corridor, then hit three balls trying only to keep them inside the fairway, ignoring distance. After each set, step back and count how many finished inside. Do 7 sets (21 balls, with a couple to settle in).

Why this drill for you

This builds the fairway finder shot you can reach for on tight holes — the one that keeps you in play instead of leaking right or left off the tee.

Score to record
/ 20 balls in corridor
2
Start-line gate — driver & iron 15 min
What to do

Stick two alignment sticks about 3 feet in front of the ball to form a gate roughly 1 yard wide, aimed at the middle of your corridor. Your only job: start the ball through the gate — don't worry about curve or distance yet. Hit 10 balls with driver, then 10 with a 6 or 7 iron (same gate, same target). Take a brief pause between each ball to reset your aim. After each shot, note if it went through the gate, missed left, or missed right.

Why this drill for you

Tee shot accuracy isn't just a driver problem — par 3s matter too. Grooving the same start-line habit across driver and iron means you're building one tee-box routine that works for every hole on your course.

9-hole round tracker on course
What to do

On your next round, bring a small notepad or use your phone's notes. On every tee shot, mark two things: (1) did you hit the fairway or green? and (2) did you use your fairway finder shot from the range? That's it — no swing thoughts, no pressure. Just observe and record.

Why this matters for you

You play nearly every day — that's a huge advantage. Most golfers only get one or two chances a week to test what they've worked on. You can start seeing results in your very next round.
